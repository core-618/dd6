# Why DD6 Instead of VUCA

## The question

VUCA (Volatility, Uncertainty, Complexity, Ambiguity) is a well-known uncertainty framework. Why not use it for problem classification?

DD6 acknowledges VUCA as theoretical inspiration but deliberately creates original dimensions.

---

## Five problems with VUCA for software discovery

### 1. Dimensions overlap

A 2022 systematic review (Emerald Publishing) concluded: "there is no clear distinction among the constructs, and they do overlap with each other."

Uncertainty and Ambiguity are hard to separate in practice. Complexity and Uncertainty confuse practitioners. If dimensions are not independent, scoring loses rigor.

DD6's six dimensions were validated for independence — each varies independently across different intake types.

### 2. Too generic

VUCA describes the world but does not prescribe action. "Your environment is volatile, uncertain, complex, and ambiguous" — and then what?

DD6 maps each score to a concrete discovery action: skip, shallow sessions, deep sessions, or emergency stabilization.

### 3. No scoring model

VUCA is purely qualitative. There is no native 1-3 scoring, no composite, no depth mapping. Any scoring applied to VUCA is an ad-hoc addition.

DD6 was designed with scoring from the start — the 1-3 scale, the 6-18 composite, and the depth thresholds are integral to the model.

### 4. Not specific to software

VUCA was created for post-Cold War military strategy (US Army War College, 1987). Adaptations for business exist, but for software discovery — and specifically for AI-assisted development — there are none.

DD6's dimensions answer questions that only matter in software: "Can an agent test this?", "Is there precedent in the codebase?", "How many stakeholders need to agree on the spec?"

### 5. BANI already proposed as successor

Jamais Cascio proposed BANI (Brittle, Anxious, Non-linear, Incomprehensible) as a modern replacement for VUCA, arguing VUCA was designed for a different era. The existence of BANI demonstrates ongoing dissatisfaction with VUCA's applicability.

---

## What DD6 keeps from VUCA

DD6 respects VUCA's insight that uncertainty is multi-dimensional. But it creates dimensions specific to the domain of AI-assisted software discovery, rather than repackaging generic dimensions.

VUCA asked: "How uncertain is the environment?"
DD6 asks: "How much does this specific problem need to be explored before an AI agent can produce a reliable spec?"
